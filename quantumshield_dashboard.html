Post-Quantum Signed Audit Log

Every sensitive action is appended to a SHA-256 hash chain and signed. Tampering with any past entry breaks the chain from that point forward — this is what makes it tamper-evident, not merely tamper-logged.
Hashing here runs live in your browser via crypto.subtle.digest('SHA-256', …) over each entry chained to the previous entry's hash — the same chain-of-custody construction the backend implements server-side in security/pqc.py. Signatures shown as SIM-HBS represent the dependency-free hash-based signer used for local development; production deployments switch PQC_BACKEND=liboqs for real NIST-standardized ML-DSA / CRYSTALS-Dilithium3 signatures.
